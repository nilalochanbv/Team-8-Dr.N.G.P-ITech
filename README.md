# Team-8-Dr.N.G.P-ITech
## Problem Statement

By 2030, Artificial Intelligence will automate many routine tasks such as coding, content creation, design assistance, and customer support. While AI increases productivity, it also creates uncertainty among students and educational institutions regarding future employability.
### Challenges Faced by Students

* Uncertainty about which skills will remain relevant in the future.
* Difficulty identifying emerging career opportunities.
* Lack of guidance on industry-required competencies.
* Limited exposure to real-world projects and future technologies.
### Challenges Faced by Colleges

* Keeping curriculum aligned with rapidly changing industry demands.
* Ensuring students remain employable in an AI-driven workforce.
* Bridging the gap between academic learning and practical industry skills.
* Providing personalized career guidance at scale.
* 
There is a need for an intelligent ecosystem that continuously helps students identify future career paths, assess skill gaps, build relevant experience, and stay employable in the evolving job market.


Simulating AI-driven hiring processes
Creating a digital employability profile for students
Providing colleges with future workforce intelligence

This gap creates an opportunity for a next-generation employability platform.

1. Project-Based Learning Module
2. Portfolio Builder
3. Employability Score Engine

Advanced modules such as AI Hiring Simulation, Mentor Marketplace, and Internship Marketplace should be introduced in later releases after validating user adoption and business viability.
